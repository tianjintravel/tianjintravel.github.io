Tianjin Accommodation: Hotels with Rooftop Bars

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • -Blog Details

Tianjin, a vibrant metropolis where history collides with modernity, offers travelers an unforgettable blend of European-inspired architecture, bustling markets, and cutting-edge skyscrapers. For those seeking a stay that combines luxury, views, and nightlife, Tianjin’s hotels with rooftop bars are the ultimate choice. Whether you’re sipping cocktails under the stars or admiring the skyline by day, these accommodations elevate your travel experience—literally.

Why Choose a Hotel with a Rooftop Bar in Tianjin?

Staying at a hotel with a rooftop bar isn’t just about convenience—it’s about immersing yourself in the city’s energy. Tianjin’s skyline, dotted with landmarks like the Tianjin Eye and the iconic Jinwan Plaza, is best appreciated from above. A rooftop bar offers:

  • Unmatched Views: Panoramic sights of the Hai River, colonial-era buildings, and futuristic towers.
  • Exclusive Nightlife: No need to venture far for a chic evening—your hotel doubles as a hotspot.
  • Instagram-Worthy Moments: Sunset cocktails against a glittering cityscape? Yes, please.

Top Tianjin Hotels with Rooftop Bars

1. The Ritz-Carlton, Tianjin

Luxury Meets Sky-High Sophistication

Perched on the upper floors of the Tianjin Global Financial Center, The Ritz-Carlton’s rooftop bar, Flair, is a magnet for discerning travelers.

Highlights:

  • 360-Degree Views: Gaze at the Hai River while lounging in plush seating.
  • Signature Cocktails: Try the Jin Hai (a local twist on the gin fizz).
  • Weekend DJ Sets: Elevate your nights with curated music.

Ideal for: Couples and luxury seekers.

2. Raffles Tianjin

Colonial Elegance with a Modern Edge

Housed in a restored historic building, Raffles Tianjin blends old-world charm with rooftop glamour at The Writers Bar.

Highlights:

  • Timeless Ambiance: Think leather armchairs and vintage chandeliers.
  • Afternoon Tea with a View: A must for culture-loving travelers.
  • Hidden Rooftop Garden: A serene escape above the city buzz.

Ideal for: History buffs and solo travelers.

3. St. Regis Tianjin

Glittering Skyline and Bespoke Service

The St. Regis’s Astor Lounge transforms into a rooftop oasis by night, offering bespoke mixology and skyline vistas.

Highlights:

  • Champagne Rituals: A nightly tradition to toast the city lights.
  • Private Cabins: For intimate gatherings or solo indulgence.
  • Local-Inspired Mixes: Try the Tianjin Mule (ginger beer meets baijiu).

Ideal for: Business travelers and celebratory stays.

4. Andaz Tianjin

Bold Design and Urban Vibes

Hyatt’s Andaz brand brings a playful, artsy vibe to Tianjin, with its rooftop bar Andaz Xian becoming a favorite among creatives.

Highlights:

  • Street Art Decor: Murals and neon lights set the scene.
  • Craft Cocktails: Infusions using Tianjin’s famed hawthorn berries.
  • Late-Night Bites: Pair drinks with Sichuan-spiced skewers.

Ideal for: Millennials and design enthusiasts.

Rooftop Bars Beyond the Hotel Scene

While hotel bars dominate, Tianjin also boasts standalone rooftop gems like Sky Lounge (near Italian Style Street) and Cloud 9 at the World Financial Center. These spots are perfect for bar-hopping after checking into a more budget-friendly hotel.

Pro Tips for Booking

  1. Season Matters: Summer and early autumn offer the best open-air experiences.
  2. Reservations: Popular bars (like Flair) book up fast—secure a spot in advance.
  3. Dress Codes: Upscale venues may require smart casual attire.

Final Thoughts

From sipping martinis at The Ritz-Carlton to unwinding in Raffles’ garden, Tianjin’s rooftop hotel bars redefine urban escapes. Whether you’re here for business, romance, or adventure, these elevated havens ensure your stay is anything but ordinary.
