Tianjin Fast Food Chains: Price Comparison

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • -Blog Details

Tianjin, a bustling port city in northern China, is known for its rich history, European-style architecture, and—most importantly—its delicious and affordable food scene. For travelers looking to explore the city without breaking the bank, fast food chains offer a convenient and budget-friendly option. Whether you're craving local flavors or familiar international bites, this price comparison guide will help you navigate Tianjin’s fast food landscape like a pro.

Why Fast Food in Tianjin?

Tianjin’s fast food scene is a mix of homegrown chains and global giants, each offering unique dishes at competitive prices. For tourists, these spots provide quick meals between sightseeing stops, especially near major attractions like the Ancient Culture Street, Five Great Avenues, or the Tianjin Eye. Plus, with many chains offering combo meals, you can enjoy a filling meal without overspending.

Local Fast Food Chains

Goubuli (狗不理包子)

A Tianjin icon, Goubuli is famous for its steamed buns (baozi). While it’s more of a sit-down restaurant than a traditional fast food chain, its quick-service locations make it a must-try for visitors.

  • Signature Dish: Pork baozi (6 pieces) – ¥30-40
  • Combo Meal: Baozi + soup – ¥50-60
  • Best For: A taste of authentic Tianjin cuisine

Yangcheng Soy Milk (永和豆浆)

A popular breakfast spot, Yangcheng serves soy milk, fried dough sticks (youtiao), and other traditional bites. It’s a great stop for an early meal before exploring the city.

  • Signature Dish: Soy milk + youtiao – ¥10-15
  • Combo Meal: Breakfast set (soy milk, youtiao, dumplings) – ¥20-25
  • Best For: Quick, hearty breakfasts

Malaxiangguo (麻辣香锅)

Though not a chain in the traditional sense, malaxiangguo (spicy stir-fry) shops are everywhere in Tianjin. You pick your ingredients, and they’re cooked in a fiery, numbing sauce.

  • Average Price: ¥30-50 per person (depending on ingredients)
  • Best For: Spicy food lovers

International Fast Food Chains

McDonald’s

McDonald’s in Tianjin offers a mix of global favorites and China-exclusive items. Prices are slightly higher than local chains but still reasonable.

  • Signature Dish: McSpicy Chicken Burger – ¥20
  • Combo Meal: Burger + fries + drink – ¥35-40
  • Best For: Familiar flavors with local twists

KFC

KFC is wildly popular in China, and Tianjin is no exception. The menu includes rice bowls, congee, and fried chicken with unique sauces.

  • Signature Dish: Original Recipe Chicken – ¥15 (per piece)
  • Combo Meal: 2-piece chicken + fries + drink – ¥30-35
  • Best For: A mix of Western and Chinese fast food

Pizza Hut

Unlike its U.S. counterpart, Pizza Hut in Tianjin leans more toward a casual dining experience, but it still has affordable lunch sets.

  • Signature Dish: Seafood Pizza (personal size) – ¥40-50
  • Combo Meal: Pizza + pasta + drink – ¥60-70
  • Best For: A slightly fancier fast-food option

Street Food vs. Fast Food Chains

While fast food chains offer consistency, Tianjin’s street food is unbeatable for flavor and value. Here’s a quick comparison:

  • Jianbing (煎饼): ¥6-10
  • Grilled Skewers (串儿): ¥2-5 per stick
  • Fried Dumplings (锅贴): ¥10-15 for 6 pieces

If you’re on a tight budget, street food wins. But for air-conditioned seating and standardized hygiene, chains are a safer bet.

Budget Tips for Travelers

  1. Combo Meals Save Money – Most chains offer discounted sets.
  2. Use Apps for Discounts – Ele.me and Meituan often have coupons.
  3. Try Local Chains First – They’re usually cheaper than international brands.
  4. Share Larger Portions – Some dishes (like malaxiangguo) are perfect for splitting.

Whether you’re grabbing a quick bite between Tianjin’s historic sites or craving a taste of home, the city’s fast food options have you covered. From steaming baozi to crispy fried chicken, there’s something for every budget and palate.
