Tianjin’s Best Golf Courses for Shanghai Players

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • -Blog Details

For Shanghai-based golf enthusiasts looking to escape the city’s hustle and bustle, Tianjin offers a surprising array of world-class golf courses. Just a short flight or high-speed train ride away, Tianjin combines rich history, modern luxury, and stunning landscapes—making it an ideal golf getaway. Whether you’re seeking championship-level challenges or serene fairways surrounded by nature, here are the top golf courses in Tianjin that Shanghai players shouldn’t miss.

Why Tianjin? A Golfer’s Hidden Gem

Tianjin might not be the first name that comes to mind for golf tourism, but it’s a rising star in China’s golf scene. With its proximity to Beijing and Shanghai, excellent transport links, and a mix of coastal and inland courses, Tianjin provides a refreshing alternative to more crowded golf destinations. The city’s cooler climate in spring and autumn also makes it perfect for comfortable rounds outside of Shanghai’s humid summers.

Easy Accessibility from Shanghai

Tianjin is just a 2-hour flight or a 5-hour high-speed train ride from Shanghai, making weekend golf trips entirely feasible. Many courses are located near the city center or within a short drive, so you can maximize your time on the greens.

Top Golf Courses in Tianjin

1. Tianjin Binhai Forest Golf Club

Best for: Championship-Level Play

Designed by renowned architect Ted Parslow, this 27-hole course is a masterpiece of strategic bunkering and undulating greens. Nestled in a forested area, the course offers a tranquil escape with challenging layouts that will test even seasoned players.

Key Features:

  • Three distinct 9-hole loops (Lake, Forest, and Meadow)
  • Host of multiple professional tournaments
  • Luxurious clubhouse with top-tier amenities

Travel Tip: Pair your golf day with a visit to the nearby Binhai New Area for its modern architecture and vibrant dining scene.

2. Tianjin Yangliuqing Golf Club

Best for: Traditionalists Who Love Classic Design

This 18-hole course blends traditional Scottish links-style elements with Tianjin’s natural beauty. Wide fairways and subtle elevation changes make it enjoyable for all skill levels, while the surrounding willow trees add a poetic touch.

Why Shanghai Players Will Love It:

  • Less crowded than Shanghai courses, offering a relaxed pace
  • Affordable green fees compared to premium Shanghai clubs
  • On-site spa and gourmet dining for post-game relaxation

3. Tianjin Dongli Lake Golf Club

Best for: Scenic Lakeside Golf

Built around the stunning Dongli Lake, this course is a visual treat. Water comes into play on nearly every hole, demanding precision and strategy. The club also offers night golfing under LED lights—a rare experience in China.

Highlights:

  • 18-hole, par-72 course designed by JMP Golf Design Group
  • Night golf available during summer months
  • Lakeside villas for overnight stays

Pro Tip: Visit in autumn for breathtaking foliage reflections on the lake.

4. Tianjin Huaming International Golf Club

Best for: Luxury and Exclusivity

This members-only club (with limited visitor access) is one of Tianjin’s most prestigious. Designed by Jack Nicklaus, the course features rolling terrain, deep bunkers, and immaculate conditioning.

What Sets It Apart:

  • Nicklaus’ signature risk-reward hole designs
  • Five-star clubhouse with fine dining and wine cellars
  • Close proximity to Tianjin’s hot springs for a post-game soak

5. Tianjin Teda Golf Club

*Best for: Coastal Golf with a Resort Vibe**

Located near the Bohai Sea, this 27-hole course offers a unique seaside golf experience. Windy conditions add an extra layer of challenge, making it a favorite among players who enjoy adapting to nature’s elements.

Don’t Miss:

  • Three distinct courses (Dunes, Lakes, and Valley)
  • Seaside resort with spa and private beach access
  • Fresh seafood restaurants nearby

Beyond Golf: Tianjin’s Must-Do Experiences

A golf trip to Tianjin isn’t complete without exploring the city’s cultural and culinary highlights. Here are a few add-ons to enhance your itinerary:

Explore the Five Great Avenues (Wuda Dao)

This historic district is a showcase of colonial-era architecture, with over 2,000 European-style buildings. Rent a bike or take a guided tour to appreciate the blend of Eastern and Western influences.

Indulge in Tianjin’s Street Food

From crispy jianbing (savory crepes) to goubuli baozi (steamed buns), Tianjin’s food scene is a delight. The Nanshi Food Street is a great starting point.

Relax at Tianjin’s Hot Springs

After a long day on the course, unwind at one of Tianjin’s many hot spring resorts, such as the Social Mountain Hot Springs or Dongli Lake Hot Springs.

Planning Your Trip

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (April-May): Mild temperatures, blooming scenery
  • Autumn (September-October): Cool weather, fewer crowds
    Avoid July-August due to heat and humidity.

Getting There

  • By Air: Direct flights from Shanghai to Tianjin Binhai International Airport (approx. 2 hours)
  • By Train: High-speed trains from Shanghai Hongqiao Station to Tianjin Station (5-6 hours)

Where to Stay

  • Luxury: The Ritz-Carlton, Tianjin (downtown)
  • Golf Resort: Tianjin Dongli Lake Golf Villas
  • Boutique: The Astor Hotel (historic charm)

Pack your clubs, book your tee times, and get ready to discover Tianjin’s golfing treasures—just a short hop from Shanghai!
